# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Professionals who negotiate, sell, or lead as part of their work — managers, entrepreneurs, salespeople — and are tired of trying to win through arguments, justification, and persuasion pressure. They land on the page already familiar with the frustration of "losing before the conversation starts" and are looking for a subtler way to influence outcomes. No confirmed secondary audience.

## Product Purpose

A sales landing page for "Карта ролей и интересов" ("Map of Roles and Interests"), a paid two-session intensive course by Olga Grishchenko (20–27 August) teaching the art of persuasion through managing conversational roles rather than arguments. Success is a visitor buying either the training seat or the license to host the intensive themselves.

## Positioning

Побеждают не те, у кого сильнее аргументы, а те, кто умеет управлять ролями в разговоре.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Купить билет (обучение на интенсиве)
- Secondary offer: Купить право ведения интенсива (отдельный продукт для тех, кто хочет проводить интенсив сам)
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: если вы устали убеждать, объяснять и давить аргументами — этот интенсив покажет, как влиять на решения людей гораздо тоньше
- Belief ladder: (1) аргументы не решают исход разговора — роль решает → (2) есть конкретная система (карта ролей: 10 ролей для себя + 10 для собеседника) → (3) систему можно освоить за два трёхчасовых занятия → (4) после курса остаётся личный инструмент (карта + конструктор) на все будущие переговоры
- Proof on hand: автор — двукратная чемпионка России по управленческой борьбе, основатель фабрики бизнес-игр F5GAME.RU, автор книги и 50 бизнес-игр (see author section); photo of Olga pending

## Brand Personality

Sharp, confident, insight-driven — a smart friend who's cracked a code everyone else is guessing at, not a corporate training brand. The provided illustrations set the visual tone already: bold, slightly comic, character-driven scenes (a man buried in arguments, sneaking past a door, a chess player), in a purple/green/magenta palette. Wit carries the persuasion, not hype.

## Anti-references

Avoid the generic, templated look of AI-generated landing pages (predictable gradient-glow hero text, stock pill badges, interchangeable SaaS-marketing layout) and also avoid generic Russian "infobiz" landing conventions — countdown-timer urgency, emoji-stuffed CAPSLOCK banners, stock photos of people pointing at charts. The existing character illustrations are the identity; lean on them instead of generic iconography.

## Design Principles

- The illustrations tell the argument: each character scene (checklist guy, door peek, boss, chess player, role map) maps to a specific story beat in the copy — place them so the visual carries the punchline, not just decoration.
- Roles over arguments, visually too: layout should feel like it's revealing a system (the role map), not stacking bullet-point claims.
- Confident restraint: sharp typography and one strong accent palette drawn from the illustrations, not a rainbow of marketing gradients.
- Two distinct offers stay visually distinct: the training seat and the "right to host" license are different products and should never be presented as if they're tiers of the same thing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific accessibility requirements stated. Follow standard good practice (readable contrast, keyboard-navigable interactive elements).
